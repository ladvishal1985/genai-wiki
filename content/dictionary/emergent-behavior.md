Emergent behavior refers to capabilities that appear suddenly in LLMs at or above a certain scale threshold—not present in smaller models, but appearing (seemingly discontinuously) in larger ones. Examples include multi-step arithmetic, chain-of-thought reasoning, and few-shot learning. The nature of emergence is debated: some researchers argue it is a genuine phase transition; others argue it is an artifact of evaluation metrics that are non-linear in task performance.

Confabulation (synonymous with hallucination in LLM contexts) refers to a model generating plausible-sounding but factually incorrect information with apparent confidence, without awareness that it is fabricating. The term is borrowed from neuroscience, where it describes patients who unconsciously fabricate memories. In LLMs it arises from the model interpolating patterns in training data rather than retrieving stored facts. Unlike sycophancy (agreeing with the user), confabulation occurs independently of user input.

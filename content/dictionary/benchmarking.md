Benchmarking evaluates model capabilities against standardised datasets and tasks, providing comparable, reproducible scores that allow ranking models and tracking progress over time. Common benchmarks include MMLU (knowledge breadth), HumanEval (code generation), GSM8K (math reasoning), HellaSwag (commonsense), and MT-Bench (instruction following). Benchmark saturation is a persistent problem — once models are trained on data resembling benchmark questions, scores inflate without reflecting real-world capability gains. Leaderboards like LMSYS Chatbot Arena (human preference voting) and HuggingFace Open LLM Leaderboard provide complementary perspectives to static dataset benchmarks.

A dense embedding is a fixed-length vector of continuous floating-point values where most dimensions are non-zero, produced by encoding text through a neural network. Dense embeddings capture semantic meaning and enable similarity search by proximity in vector space. Produced by bi-encoder models (e.g., sentence-transformers), they are the dominant representation used in modern RAG retrieval and vector databases. Contrast with sparse embeddings, where most dimensions are zero.

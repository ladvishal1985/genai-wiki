A memory retrieval policy defines the rules for deciding when and what to retrieve from long-term memory into the active context. Policies may be time-based (retrieve recent memories), relevance-based (retrieve by embedding similarity to current query), importance-weighted, or a combination. A poor retrieval policy floods the context with irrelevant memories or misses critical information. Designing retrieval policies is analogous to cache eviction policy design in systems engineering.

A sparse embedding is a high-dimensional vector (typically vocabulary-sized) where most values are zero, capturing lexical or term-frequency information. Classical sparse representations include TF-IDF and BM25 vectors. Learned sparse methods like SPLADE produce sparse vectors through neural models. Sparse embeddings excel at exact keyword matching and complement dense embeddings in hybrid search systems.

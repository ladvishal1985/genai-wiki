Role prompting assigns the LLM a persona or role at the start of a conversation ("You are an expert cardiologist", "You are a strict code reviewer") to influence its tone, expertise level, and response framing. Role assignment leverages the model's ability to condition on persona context from pretraining. While effective for style and tone, role prompting does not grant factual knowledge the model doesn't have, and overly rigid personas can cause the model to refuse helpful out-of-role responses.

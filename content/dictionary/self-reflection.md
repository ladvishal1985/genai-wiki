Self-reflection in LLM agents is the process of the agent evaluating its own intermediate outputs, plans, or actions before proceeding. The agent is prompted (or trained) to critique its reasoning, check for errors, verify tool call results, and reconsider its approach. Self-reflection adds a metacognitive loop that catches mistakes before they compound across multiple steps in long agentic tasks. It is more lightweight than Reflexion (which stores reflections across episodes) and operates within a single task.

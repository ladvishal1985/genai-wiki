Multimodal models process and generate content across multiple data modalities — typically combinations of text, images, audio, and video — within a unified architecture. Early multimodal systems used separate encoders per modality with cross-attention bridges; modern approaches like GPT-4V, Gemini, and Claude embed all modalities into a shared token space the language model decoder can attend over. Capabilities include image understanding, visual question answering, chart and diagram interpretation, and interleaved image-text generation. Multimodal models are increasingly the default for foundation models, as real-world tasks rarely involve text alone.

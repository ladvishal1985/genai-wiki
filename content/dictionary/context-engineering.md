Context engineering is the discipline of deliberately constructing the content injected into an LLM's context window—deciding what information to include, how to order it, how to compress it, and how to budget the available tokens. It treats the context window as a scarce resource to be managed. Context engineering subsumes prompt engineering and extends it to dynamic, retrieval-augmented, and multi-turn settings where the right context makes the difference between a correct and an incorrect response.

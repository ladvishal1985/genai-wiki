Scaling laws are empirical relationships that describe how LLM performance (measured by test loss) improves predictably as a power law with increases in model parameters, training data tokens, and compute budget. Kaplan et al. (2020) established the foundational scaling laws; the Chinchilla paper (2022) revised them to show that most models were undertrained—optimal performance requires scaling data proportional to parameters. Scaling laws guide hardware and training budget allocation decisions.

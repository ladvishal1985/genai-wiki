Human-in-the-loop (HITL) is the pattern of requiring human review or approval at critical decision points in an agentic pipeline—before irreversible actions (sending emails, making purchases, deleting data), on low-confidence decisions, or at configurable checkpoints. HITL provides a safety valve against autonomous agent mistakes and is essential for high-stakes applications. The trade-off is latency and reduced autonomy; the goal is to place checkpoints where the cost of a mistake outweighs the benefit of automation.

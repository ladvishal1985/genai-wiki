Open-weight models are LLMs whose trained weights are publicly released (e.g., Llama, Mistral, Gemma, Falcon), allowing anyone to download, run, and fine-tune them. Distinct from "open source" (which implies training code and data access), open-weight means only the weights are available. Open-weight models enable self-hosted deployment (data privacy, no API costs), fine-tuning on proprietary data, and integration into products without terms-of-service restrictions. The trade-off versus closed-API models is self-managed infrastructure and generally lower out-of-box capability at equivalent scales.

A prompt is the input text (and optionally images or other modalities) provided to a language model to elicit a desired response. Prompts range from a simple question to complex multi-paragraph instructions containing background context, output format specifications, examples, and constraints. The quality and structure of the prompt is the primary lever available to practitioners who cannot modify model weights — a well-crafted prompt can transform a mediocre response into an excellent one. Prompt engineering is the discipline of systematically designing, testing, and optimising prompts for reliability, accuracy, and safety.

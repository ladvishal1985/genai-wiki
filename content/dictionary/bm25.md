BM25 (Best Match 25) is a probabilistic ranking function used in lexical search that scores documents based on term frequency, inverse document frequency, and document length normalisation. It is the standard algorithm underlying Elasticsearch, OpenSearch, and Solr. BM25 rewards documents that contain query terms frequently and penalises very long documents where terms may appear by chance. While it cannot handle paraphrase or semantic similarity, it is exceptionally good at matching specific identifiers, product codes, proper nouns, and technical terms — making it a critical complement to dense retrieval in hybrid search systems.

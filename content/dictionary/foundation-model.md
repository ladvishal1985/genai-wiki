A foundation model is a large model trained on broad, diverse data at scale that can be adapted to a wide range of downstream tasks through fine-tuning, prompting, or in-context learning. The term, coined by Stanford's HAI in 2021, captures the idea that such models serve as a general-purpose "foundation" rather than being purpose-built for a single task. Foundation models exhibit emergent capabilities — skills not explicitly trained for that appear at sufficient scale — and benefit from transfer learning, where representations learned during pre-training generalise across domains. The category encompasses language models, vision models, and multimodal models.

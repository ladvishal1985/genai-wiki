Dimensionality reduction is the process of projecting high-dimensional embedding vectors into a lower-dimensional space while preserving as much useful structure as possible. Techniques include PCA (linear), UMAP (non-linear), and learned projection heads. In retrieval systems it reduces storage cost and speeds up ANN search. Trade-offs involve loss of information and potential degradation in recall.

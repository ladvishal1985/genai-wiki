The latent space is the high-dimensional geometric space in which a neural model's internal representations live. Points that are semantically similar are positioned close together. In the context of embeddings, the latent space defines the coordinate system where retrieval, clustering, and analogy operations occur. Understanding the geometry of the latent space—how concepts cluster, spread, or collapse—is essential for diagnosing embedding quality and designing retrieval pipelines.

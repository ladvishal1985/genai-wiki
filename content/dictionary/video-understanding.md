Video understanding refers to AI tasks that process video content—temporal sequences of frames with audio—to answer questions, generate summaries, or extract structured information. Challenges include handling long temporal contexts (videos are far more tokens than images), capturing motion and causality, and synchronizing visual and audio streams. Models like Gemini 1.5 and VideoLLaMA process video frames with temporal attention. Video understanding is nascent in RAG pipelines but increasingly relevant for multimedia knowledge bases.
